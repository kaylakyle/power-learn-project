The Big Idea

AI is like a super smart but very literal child.

If you ask:

"Help me sell more products."

The AI might say:

"Use social media."

"Do marketing."

That's not very helpful.

But if you ask:

"You are a marketing expert. I sell solar pumps in Rwanda. Farmers buy mostly during the dry season and many use SACCO loans. Give me a 3-month marketing plan."

Now AI knows exactly what you want.

So:

❌ Vague questions = Vague answers

✅ Specific questions = Better answers

1. AIM Framework

Think of AIM as giving AI a job.

A = Actor

Tell AI who it should be.

Example:

"You are a doctor."

or

"You are a software engineer."

I = Input

Give AI information.

Example:

"My website is slow."

M = Mission

Tell AI exactly what to do.

Example:

"Find 3 reasons why my website is slow and how to fix them."

Example

Bad:

Tell me about marketing.

Good:

You are a marketing manager. I own a beauty shop in Nairobi. Create a 30-day marketing plan to get 100 new customers.

2. MAP Framework

MAP helps AI understand the situation.

M = Memory

Things AI already knows.

Example:

We already talked about my beauty business.

A = Assets

Things you give AI.

Examples:

Files
Images
Excel sheets
Documents
P = Prompt/Actions

What you want AI to do.

Example:

Analyze this sales report and tell me why sales dropped.

Simple Example

Bad:

How should I price my product?

Good:

My product costs KSh 500 to make. Competitors sell at KSh 800. Give me 3 pricing options and expected profits.

3. Debugging AI

Sometimes AI gives a bad answer.

Instead of saying:

AI is useless.

You improve your prompt.

Three tricks:

Chain of Thought

Tell AI:

Think step by step.

This makes AI explain its thinking.

Verifier Pattern

Tell AI:

Ask me 3 questions before answering.

This helps AI understand you first.

Refinement

Tell AI:

Make my prompt better.

AI will improve your question.

4. OCEAN Framework

AI sometimes makes things up.

Before believing it, check:

O = Original

Is it a fresh idea?

C = Concrete

Does it use real examples and numbers?

E = Evidence

Can it prove what it says?

A = Assertive

Does it clearly state its opinion?

N = Narrative

Does it explain things in a way people understand?

5. AI Flywheel (Automation)

Imagine a restaurant.

Normally:

You take orders → Cook → Serve → Get paid

One person doing everything gets tired.

A flywheel is when tools work together automatically.

Example:

Find customers
Send emails automatically
Create proposals automatically
Collect payments automatically

Less work for you.

More money.
